The Mystical World of Mushrooms

Mushrooms are incredible little organisms that populate the dark shadowy spots of the world's woods and forests. Few types of plants are so inriguing, and each type has their own sort of style, flavor, and personality; just like people. It makes sense that they are genetically closer to humans than other types of plants. Let's go exploring in our little forest here and see what kinds of mushrooms we can find...

What are these little yellow things?

These are Chantrelle mushrooms! They are a delicious variety of edible muchrooms with a floral scent and a peppery taste. They are so cute the author has a tattoo of them even!

Is that a piece of honeycomb?

No, they're not; in fact these are Morel mushrooms! They are an edible muchroom, coveted for their deep meaty taste. They are best sauted in butter.

Ooo! It's so pretty!

Whoa! Be careful, those are fly agaric mushrooms, also known as amanita muscaria. They can only be eaten after special preparations, and if eaten without those they are extremely poisonous. A good rule of thumb: don't eat mushrooms you picked yourself unless you're sure what they are!

What are these weird globs?

These aren't globs! They're a type of mushroom known as "Hen of the Woods", also referred to as miatake, meaning "dancing mushroom" in Japanese. They are great in stir fry.

I didn't know the forest had bean sprouts!

Those aren't bean sprouts silly! Those are a type of mushroom called enoki, commonly used in Asian cooking. They are much more flavorful than beansprouts and much more versatile in the kitchen.
